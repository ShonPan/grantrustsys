**Title:** A Hybrid Graph Neural Network Framework for High-Resolution Mapping of Cell-Cell Communication Networks in the Tumor Microenvironment

The tumor microenvironment (TME) plays a critical role in cancer progression and response to immunotherapy. Existing computational tools for inferring cell-cell interactions from omics data often lack spatial context and integrative power. We hypothesize that a novel hybrid model combining graph attention networks (GATs) with variational autoencoders (VAEs) and transformer encoders will accurately predict ligand-receptor interactions and downstream signaling cascades by integrating single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq) and spatial transcriptomics data. This approach will reveal hidden mechanisms of immune evasion in solid tumors.

**Specific Aims and Methods**

In Aim 1, we will develop robust data integration pipelines. We will acquire and preprocess datasets from public repositories, applying batch effect correction using Harmony and normalization via log-transformation and scaling in Scanpy. Spatial data will undergo deconvolution using a custom deep learning module based on conditional VAEs to assign cell types to spots with high precision. Feature selection will employ mutual information criteria to prioritize ligand-receptor pairs from databases such as CellPhoneDB and STRING.

Aim 2 will focus on model architecture and training. We will construct intercellular graphs where nodes represent cells or spots and edges encode potential interactions weighted by expression levels and spatial proximity. A multi-layer GAT will propagate information across the graph, with attention heads learning context-specific importance of interactions. Multi-omics features will be embedded using transformer layers for sequence-derived motifs and pathway annotations. The VAE component will learn low-dimensional representations for denoising and imputation of missing values. Training will proceed in phases: pre-training on simulated networks generated via stochastic block models, followed by fine-tuning on real datasets using Adam optimizer with learning rate scheduling. Regularization via dropout and L2 penalties will prevent overfitting. We will implement 10-fold cross-validation stratified by cancer type and evaluate using ROC-AUC, precision-recall curves, and downstream biological coherence scores derived from Gene Ontology enrichment.

In Aim 3, we will apply the framework to clinical cohorts of melanoma and lung adenocarcinoma. In silico perturbation analysis will simulate therapeutic interventions by node removal, predicting shifts in network topology using metrics such as betweenness centrality and modularity. Benchmarking against tools like LIANA and NicheNet will demonstrate superior performance. Predicted novel interactions will undergo structural validation via molecular docking simulations using AutoDock.

**Significance and Expected Outcomes**

This project will provide an open-source Python package, generate testable hypotheses for experimental follow-up, and contribute to more effective immunotherapies by identifying druggable pathways and improved patient stratification models. Upon completion, the framework will advance precision oncology by enabling proactive design of combination therapies that disrupt immune-evasive communication networks, ultimately improving clinical outcomes for patients with treatment-resistant cancers.
