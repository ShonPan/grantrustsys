The proliferation of artificial intelligence systems across critical domains necessitates the development of comprehensive evaluation frameworks that can systematically assess alignment with human values and intentions. Current methodologies for AI safety assessment remain fragmented, lacking standardized benchmarks that enable consistent measurement of alignment properties across diverse model architectures and deployment contexts. This proposal outlines a systematic approach to developing robust evaluation frameworks that address fundamental gaps in contemporary AI safety research.

Key insight: Existing alignment evaluation methods focus primarily on behavioral outputs rather than underlying representational structures that drive decision-making processes. This creates critical blind spots. Our framework addresses this through multi-layered assessment protocols.

The proposed research program will establish standardized methodologies for evaluating AI system alignment through the development of comprehensive benchmark suites that incorporate both behavioral and mechanistic interpretability measures. These evaluation frameworks will be designed to assess the degree to which AI systems maintain alignment with specified objectives across various perturbation scenarios, including distribution shifts, adversarial inputs, and novel task domains that extend beyond training distributions.

The technical approach encompasses three primary components:
• Development of behavioral alignment benchmarks measuring consistency between stated objectives and observed system behaviors
• Integration of mechanistic interpretability techniques to assess internal representational alignment
• Creation of robustness evaluation protocols examining alignment preservation under various stress conditions

Methodological innovation centers on the integration of formal verification techniques with empirical evaluation approaches, enabling both theoretical guarantees and practical assessment capabilities. The proposed benchmark suite will incorporate diverse evaluation scenarios ranging from simple preference learning tasks to complex multi-stakeholder environments where competing objectives must be balanced according to predetermined ethical frameworks and value hierarchies.

Expected outcomes include the establishment of standardized evaluation protocols that can be adopted across research institutions and industry organizations, facilitating reproducible assessment of AI safety properties. The resulting benchmarks will enable systematic comparison of different alignment approaches, accelerating progress toward more reliable and beneficial AI systems through improved evaluation methodologies.

Key insight: Standardization enables comparative analysis. Different research groups currently use incompatible evaluation methods. This makes progress assessment nearly impossible.

The broader impact of this research extends beyond technical contributions to encompass policy and governance implications, as standardized evaluation frameworks provide essential foundations for regulatory oversight and certification processes. The development of these benchmarks will inform evidence-based approaches to AI governance, enabling more effective risk assessment and mitigation strategies across deployment contexts.

Implementation will involve collaboration with leading research institutions and industry partners to ensure benchmark relevance and adoption across diverse stakeholder communities. The proposed evaluation frameworks will be made publicly available through open-source platforms, promoting widespread adoption and continuous improvement through community contributions and feedback mechanisms.

This research addresses critical gaps in current AI safety evaluation methodologies by providing systematic approaches to alignment assessment that incorporate both behavioral and mechanistic evaluation dimensions. The resulting frameworks will establish essential infrastructure for advancing AI safety research and supporting evidence-based approaches to AI governance and deployment decisions.

The proposed work represents a foundational contribution to AI safety research that will enable more reliable assessment of alignment properties and support the development of increasingly beneficial AI systems across diverse application domains.
