It is widely acknowledged in the literature that current approaches to computational analysis of complex systems suffer from fundamental limitations in scalability, interpretability, and generalizability. This proposal presents a comprehensive research program designed to address these interrelated challenges through a unified theoretical framework.

The proposed research agenda comprises three interconnected work packages. Work Package 1 focuses on the development of scalable algorithms that can process large-scale heterogeneous datasets in real time. Work Package 2 addresses the interpretability challenge through the development of novel explanation methods. Work Package 3 integrates the outputs of the first two packages into a cohesive toolkit for end-user deployment.

The principal investigator has extensive experience in the relevant domains, having published over twenty papers in the field and supervised numerous graduate students. The research will be conducted in collaboration with leading international research groups, ensuring access to complementary expertise and datasets.

Expected outcomes include peer-reviewed publications in high-impact journals, the development of an open-source software library, and the establishment of a benchmark dataset that will serve as a community resource for future research in this domain.
