## Grant Proposal: Investigating Subsurface Cryovolcanic Activity on the Jovian Moon Aetheria

**Project Title:** Thermal Characterization and Flux Modeling of the Zephyros Chasmata  
**Principal Investigator:** Dr. Aris Thorne  
**Affiliation:** Institute for Outer Planetary Studies  
**Funding Opportunity:** Deep Space Exploration & Planetary Habitability Grant (DSEPH-2026)

---

### I. Executive Summary

This proposal seeks funding for a 24-month computational and analytical study titled **"Thermal Characterization and Flux Modeling of the Zephyros Chasmata."** The project focuses on Aetheria, a recently categorized Jovian moon characterized by high-albedo surface ice and deep tectonic fracturing. Preliminary remote sensing data from the *Vanguard-9* flyby indicates localized thermal anomalies within the Zephyros Chasmata—a network of canyons stretching over 800 kilometers. 

The primary goal of this research is to determine whether these anomalies are the result of active cryovolcanic venting or seasonal tidal dissipation. By developing high-resolution thermal inertia models, this project will provide critical insights into the moon's internal energy budget and its potential for maintaining subsurface liquid water.

---

### II. Research Objectives

The investigation is structured around three core scientific objectives:

1.  **Thermal Mapping:** To synthesize multi-spectral data into a high-resolution (50m/pixel) thermal map of the Zephyros Chasmata.
2.  **Heat Transport Modeling:** To execute Monte Carlo simulations to differentiate between conductive heat loss through the crust and convective heat transport via volatile plumes.
3.  **Habitability Assessment:** To estimate the depth of the ice-ocean interface based on the calculated heat flux, establishing a baseline for future biological life-detection missions.

---

### III. Methodology and Technical Approach

The research will utilize a dual-phase methodology combining archival data analysis with novel predictive modeling.

* **Phase A: Data Harmonization.** We will utilize raw bolometric data and infrared spectra to calculate surface temperature variations across a full Aetherian diurnal cycle. By accounting for surface roughness and regolith porosity, we will isolate "endogenic heat" from solar absorption.
* **Phase B: Numerical Simulations.** Using the *CryoSim 4.0* framework, the team will model the behavior of ammonia-water brines under the specific gravitational and atmospheric pressures of Aetheria.

| Milestone | Activity | Expected Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Q1-Q2** | Data Calibration | Refined thermal baseline for Jovian icy moons. |
| **Q3-Q4** | Simulation Runs | Identification of primary heat transport mechanisms. |
| **Q5-Q8** | Synthesis & Reporting | Publication of the Aetheria Thermal Atlas. |

---

### IV. Significance and Impact

Understanding the thermodynamic state of Aetheria is pivotal for the next decade of planetary science. If the Zephyros anomalies are confirmed as active cryovolcanoes, Aetheria would join a select group of "geologically alive" bodies in our solar system. 

This project will directly support the development of future lander instrumentation by identifying "hot spots" where the ice shell may be thinnest, thereby minimizing the energy requirements for future sub-surface drilling.

---

### V. Budget Overview

The total requested funding of **$485,000** will cover:
* **Personnel:** Support for one Principal Investigator and two Doctoral Research Assistants.
* **Computing:** High-performance computing (HPC) allocations for complex fluid dynamics simulations.
* **Dissemination:** Presentation of findings at the 2027 International Planetary Science Congress and publication in peer-reviewed journals.

Through this rigorous investigation, we aim to transform our understanding of Aetheria from a static icy sphere into a primary candidate for deep-space exploration.
