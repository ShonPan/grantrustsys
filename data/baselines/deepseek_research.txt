**Project Title:** A Multi-Omics Integrative Framework for Deciphering Non-Coding Regulatory Networks in Complex Disease Phenotypes

**Principal Investigator:** [Name]
**Submitted to:** [Agency Name]

**Project Summary**
This proposal outlines a computational framework to test the hypothesis that disease-associated non-coding variants exert pleiotropic effects through the disruption of higher-order chromatin architecture and tissue-specific transcriptional cascades. Current genome-wide association studies (GWAS) have identified risk loci predominantly in non-coding regions, yet the mechanistic link between these variants and disease pathophysiology remains largely inferential. To address this gap, we will develop and validate a novel integrative algorithm—**RegNet-3D**—that synthesizes single-cell chromatin conformation capture (scHi-C) data, epigenomic landscapes, and population-scale transcriptomic datasets. By moving beyond static linear genomics, this project will establish a predictive model for regulatory dysfunction, with the potential to identify novel therapeutic targets for complex diseases.

**Specific Aims**
**Aim 1: To construct a high-resolution, tissue-specific atlas of non-coding regulatory domains.**
We will employ a machine learning architecture—specifically a graph neural network (GNN)—to integrate scHi-C contact matrices with histone modification (ChIP-seq) and chromatin accessibility (ATAC-seq) profiles. The model will be trained to classify "enhancer-promoter interaction pairs" based on sequence features and epigenetic context. Unlike existing methods that rely on bulk tissue averages, this approach will resolve regulatory logic at single-cell resolution, enabling the identification of cell-type-specific regulatory circuits that are masked in heterogeneous tissue samples.

**Aim 2: To functionally annotate disease-associated non-coding variants within 3D chromatin networks.**
We will overlay fine-mapped GWAS summary statistics for [Disease Model] onto the regulatory atlases constructed in Aim 1. Using a novel variant effect scoring system—**VES-3D**—we will quantify the predicted impact of single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) on transcription factor binding affinity and chromatin loop stability. Variants will be prioritized not by linear proximity to genes, but by their potential to disrupt physical interactions between distal enhancers and their cognate promoters. This aim will generate a ranked list of putative causal variants and their predicted effector genes for subsequent experimental validation.

**Aim 3: To validate the predictive capacity of the RegNet-3D framework using CRISPRi interference screens.**
To empirically benchmark our computational predictions, we will collaborate to utilize existing CRISPR interference (CRISPRi) datasets targeting predicted enhancer regions. We will apply our framework to these publicly available datasets to test the correlation between our predicted *in silico* perturbation effects and empirically measured changes in transcriptional output. This validation step is critical to establish the generalizability of the model across different cellular contexts and genetic backgrounds.

**Research Strategy**
*Significance:* The central barrier to translating genomic discovery into clinical insight is the inability to functionally interpret non-coding variation. Current computational tools treat the genome as a one-dimensional sequence, ignoring the spatial architecture that dictates gene regulation. This project is significant because it will provide a mechanistic framework for interpreting the "dark matter" of the genome, shifting the paradigm from correlation to causation in genetic studies of disease.

*Innovation:* This proposal is innovative in its application of geometric deep learning to multi-modal single-cell data. By explicitly modeling the 3D genome as a graph structure, our methodology captures the non-linear relationships between genetic variation and gene expression that are inaccessible to linear regression models (e.g., PrediXcan, FOCUS). Furthermore, the development of the VES-3D scoring system represents a conceptual advance over current annotation tools, which fail to account for the context-dependence of regulatory element function.

*Approach:* In the first year, we will curate and harmonize scHi-C, ATAC-seq, and RNA-seq datasets from public repositories (ENCODE, 4DN) across three distinct tissue types. Data processing will utilize a standardized Snakemake pipeline to ensure reproducibility. The GNN model will be implemented in PyTorch Geometric, utilizing a message-passing framework to aggregate information from neighboring genomic loci. Model performance will be assessed via cross-validation against validated enhancer-gene pairs. In the second year, we will apply the trained model to GWAS data, performing fine-mapping to isolate the 95% credible set of causal variants. The VES-3D scores will be calculated by perturbing the graph structure *in silico* to simulate the effect of each variant. Finally, we will benchmark these predictions against existing CRISPRi data using receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curves and precision-recall metrics. All code and processed data will be deposited in a publicly accessible GitHub repository and Zenodo upon project completion.

**Conclusion**
Successful completion of this project will yield a scalable, open-source tool for the mechanistic interpretation of non-coding variation. By integrating the 3D genome into disease modeling, this work will provide the scientific community with a framework to prioritize drug targets with greater precision, ultimately accelerating the translation of genomic data into therapeutic strategies.
